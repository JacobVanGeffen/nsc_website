About Us

The community involvement committee showcases the College of Natural Sciences’ altruism through doing numerous volunteer works throughout the semester. Our weekly volunteer events are Kids Who Code and Young Scientists which are both centered around educating the underprivileged youth of Austin. In addition to that we are involved on the UT campus through school sponsored events like Explore UT, Longhorn Halloween, Longhorn School bus, and NS Week.

Young Scientists

Young Scientists is an educational outreach program hosted by Natural Sciences Council whose purpose is to expose underprivileged students to the fascinating world of science. This year, we are working with 6th graders at KIPP Austin Collegiate Preparatory, where we involve students in hands-on experiments and introduce them to new scientific topics. You don't want to miss out!

Kids Who Code

Kids Who Code is a weekly outreach program partnered with an eighth grade class at KIPP Collegiate Prep, a public charter school in low-income East Austin. In this program, we facilitate “unplugged’ group activities to teach the basic computer science concepts such as Boolean logic and binary.
